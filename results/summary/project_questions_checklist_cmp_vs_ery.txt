Project Questions Checklist: CMP vs Erythroblast
===============================================

Q1. What genes are differentially expressed across this cell-line pair?
Status: COMPLETE
Evidence files: results/deseq2/de_genes_deseq2.csv; results/limma_voom/de_genes_limma_voom.csv; results/summary/rna_report_summary.txt
Conclusion: Strong differential expression is detected by both methods (DESeq2: 4799; limma-voom: 5057 at adjusted p-value < 0.05 and |logFC| > 1), indicating a robust transcriptional shift between CMP and Erythroblast.

Q2. What are the functions of genes with differential expression?
Status: COMPLETE
Evidence files: results/deseq2/go_enrichment_deseq2.csv; results/limma_voom/go_enrichment_limma_voom.csv; results/summary/rna_report_summary.txt
Conclusion: RNA GO enrichment highlights erythrocyte differentiation/homeostasis and biosynthetic/ribosome programs, consistent with erythroid maturation biology.

Q3. How consistent are DESeq2 and limma-voom results?
Status: COMPLETE
Evidence files: results/summary/shared_de_genes_comparison.csv; results/summary/rna_report_summary.txt
Conclusion: Methods are highly consistent for this pair (4209 shared DE genes; 100% direction agreement), supporting stable RNA-level conclusions.

Q4. What is the RNA-seq sample relationship from clustering/tree analysis?
Status: COMPLETE (pairwise + all-cell context)
Evidence files: results/deseq2/pca_deseq2.pdf; results/deseq2/hclust_deseq2.pdf; results/limma_voom/pca_limma_voom.pdf; results/clustering/q4_rna_all_cells/sample_hclust.pdf
Conclusion: Replicates cluster by condition, and CMP separates from Erythroblast while all-cell RNA relationships preserve expected lineage structure.

Q5. What regions show differential chromatin accessibility, and what genes are nearby?
Status: COMPLETE (exploratory ATAC mode)
Evidence files: results/atac_seq/pairwise_cmp_vs_ery/DARs_consensus.csv; results/atac_seq/pairwise_cmp_vs_ery/DARs_annotated.csv; results/atac_seq/pairwise_cmp_vs_ery/README.txt
Conclusion: Differentially accessible regions were identified and annotated to nearby genes, but current inferential strength is limited by bigBed score-derived quantification.

Q6. How are differential chromatin patterns related to nearby gene expression?
Status: COMPLETE (exploratory integration mode)

Conclusion: Direction-concordant overlap exists (up/open in Ery: 629 genes; up/open in CMP: 1043 genes), supporting regulatory linkage hypotheses while remaining exploratory under current ATAC quantification.

Q7. What are the functions of genes near differential chromatin regions?

Q8. What does the ATAC hierarchical tree show, and is it similar to RNA?
Status: COMPLETE (comparison included, interpretation cautious)
